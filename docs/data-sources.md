# Data sources

Every number enklayve shows is computed from a **bundled, versioned dataset** with a full citation — never fetched at runtime (the CSP sets `connect-src 'none'`) and never guessed. This document lists the sources, their refresh cadence, and how the data layer keeps them honest.

The authoritative, machine-readable record is [`data/manifest.json`](../data/manifest.json): every shard is pinned there to a version, an effective year, a source URL, a source document name, a retrieval date, and a SHA-256 content hash. The build embeds the manifest so the running app knows exactly what it is computing from, and the release audit (`npm run audit`) fails the build if any shard is missing a citation (the no-orphan-numbers rule, SPEC §9).

> The ACA **county benchmark (second-lowest-cost silver) premium** and a Social Security **PIA** are deliberately *not* bundled — the per-county SLCSP table is enormous and changes annually. Those tools have the user supply that one local figure (pointed to HealthCare.gov / their SSA statement) so every *shipped* number stays verifiable. The **FAFSA Student Aid Index + Pell Grant** tables (ED SAI Formula Guide) are seeded from the **2026-27 dependent-student methodology** — the exact Table A2 income-protection allowances and Table A5 AAI-assessment schedule, extracted from the official guide and cited; the tools frame the result as an estimate to verify against the guide and the user's FAFSA Submission Summary (the independent-student variant and per-state aid stay out of scope).

no personal exemption, and married-filing-separately mapped to single at launch fidelity), and RI (three rates 3.75%/4.75%/5.99% that are uniform across filing statuses — only the standard deduction $11,200/$22,400/$16,800 and the $5,250-per-taxpayer personal exemption differ by status — all the 2026 inflation-adjusted figures from RI Division of Taxation Advisory ADV 2025-22; the standard-deduction/exemption phase-out above $261,000 AGI and the dependency exemption omitted at launch fidelity), and SC (H.4216, 2026: two uniform rates 1.99%/5.21% over $30,000 of South Carolina taxable income, starting from federal AGI over the new South Carolina Income Adjusted Deduction (SCIAD) $15,000/$30,000/$22,500 — a *sliding* deduction that phases down with AGI (single $40k–$95k, HoH $60k–$142.5k, joint $80k–$190k), modeled via the engine's new `standardDeductionPhaseOut` capability; the dependent exemption omitted at launch fidelity), and OK (HB 2764, 2026: three brackets over a 0% band — single/MFS 2.5%/3.5%/4.5% over $3,750/$4,900/$7,200, with married jointly / head of household / surviving spouse on a doubled schedule over $7,500/$9,800/$14,400, so head of household uses the joint rate schedule; the frozen $6,350/$12,700/$9,350 standard deduction and $1,000-per-exemption stack apply, and a future trigger-based 0.25% rate cut is the reviewer's data-only step) — for **38 jurisdictions**. No-income-tax states are first-class records, not omissions (NH's citation notes its Interest & Dividends Tax was repealed effective 2025; WA's that its capital-gains excise tax is separate from wage income; TN's that the Hall investment-income tax was repealed in 2021). Each shard is modeled at the engine's launch fidelity — brackets, standard deduction, and personal exemption (plus Utah's taxpayer tax credit), cross-checked against the Tax Foundation's 2026 state-rate table and cited to the state Department of Revenue — with state-specific credits, county/municipal add-ons (e.g. Indiana counties), and itemized deductions deferred to a later wave. Utah's relief is the nonrefundable taxpayer tax credit (6% of the federal deduction, phasing out at 1.3% above a filing-status base), now modeled via the engine's `taxpayerCredit` feature; its per-dependent exemption is omitted (the no-dependent assumption), so the figure errs slightly high.

Each shard has a sibling `.sha256` and an entry in the manifest. See [`adding-a-state.md`](adding-a-state.md) to add a jurisdiction and [`contributing.md`](contributing.md) for the workflow.

## The fail-safe contract (SPEC §7.3)

A refresh job fetches the source, parses it with a source-specific adapter, emits normalized JSON plus a content hash, appends a human-readable diff, and runs the full golden suite. It opens a PR **only if tests pass and values changed**; if a source 404s or fails schema validation it opens an *alert* PR that flips the affected rules into fail-safe mode rather than shipping a wrong number. Data is never auto-committed to `main` without passing the test gate. At runtime, a shard whose effective year is older than its refresh window — or whose hash fails — is marked **stale**, and the tiles that depend on it surface a "verify before relying" banner instead of presenting a number as current.

This contract is implemented under [`scripts/refresh/`](../scripts/refresh/): a pure, unit-tested harness (`contract.ts`: the diff and the open-PR-vs-alert decision), the source adapters (`adapters.ts`: IRS, BLS CPI, SSA, HHS, the standard-deduction states California / New York / Georgia / North Carolina / DC, the flat-rate states Pennsylvania / Illinois / Michigan / Arizona / Colorado / Indiana / Kentucky / Idaho, the graduated bracket-table states Ohio / Mississippi (a two-tier "0% then a flat rate over a floor" schedule), the special-case Massachusetts (a dedicated parser for its 5% base rate plus the 4% surtax over an inflation-adjusted threshold), the TreasuryDirect I-bond rates, USDA SNAP, and CMS Medicaid — each anchoring to known labels and flagging rather than guessing when a layout changes), and the runner (`run.ts`). With this set, **every seeded jurisdiction with an income tax has a refresh adapter.** One GitHub Actions workflow per source group (`.github/workflows/refresh-*.yml`) runs on the cadence above and on manual dispatch, gates every data PR on the golden suite, and writes each change to the [source diff log](source-diff-log.md). The adapters refresh the committed figures in place; rolling a shard to a new effective year and transcribing a full bracket table remain the reviewer's data-only step on the resulting PR (see [`adding-a-state.md`](adding-a-state.md)).
